# The city's own land

An interactive map of the **17,298 parcels New York City owns or leases**, sorted by how ready each site is to become housing.

Inspired by the NYU Furman Center's [interactive maps of city-owned land](https://www.furmancenter.org/publication/what-new-interactive-maps-reveal-about-the-development-potential-of-new-york-citys-city-owned-land/) and the Mamdani administration's LIFT task force goal of building 25,000 homes on public land.

**Live map:** https://joshgreenman1973.github.io/nyc-city-land-map/

## What it shows

Every city-owned or leased tax lot, grouped into four categories by development readiness:

- **No current use** — vacant land and unused structures (the clearest housing opportunity)
- **Already residential** — public housing and other homes on city land
- **Other active use** — offices, schools, depots, courts, infrastructure
- **Parks & recreation** — parks, playgrounds and ballfields, mostly protected

Filter by borough, agency, minimum lot size, whether a lot is zoned for housing as-of-right, and whether it's flagged for potential disposition. Click any parcel for its lot size, zoning, and a rough as-of-right housing capacity estimate.

## Data

- **City Owned and Leased Properties (COLP)** — NYC Dept. of City Planning, [`fn4k-qyk2`](https://data.cityofnewyork.us/dataset/fn4k-qyk2)
- **MapPLUTO** — NYC Dept. of City Planning, [`64uk-42ks`](https://data.cityofnewyork.us/dataset/64uk-42ks) — joined on BBL for lot size and zoning

Both via NYC Open Data, current as of July 2026. See [methodology.html](methodology.html) for categories, the capacity formula, and caveats.
